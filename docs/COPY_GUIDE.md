# Copy & Voice Guide

## The voice in one sentence

The founder of Tradie Systems Co is an ex-tradie who got sick of watching mates lose jobs because they were too flat-out to chase quotes. He's not a marketer. He talks straight, knows the work, and has zero time for jargon.

Every line of copy on the site should sound like it could come out of his mouth at smoko.

---

## Core principles

1. **Speak to the pain, not the product.** Visitors don't care about "automation". They care about losing the $14k landscaping job to the bloke who called the customer back faster.
2. **Show you understand the work.** Reference real moments: quoting at 7pm after a 10-hour day. Texts that go unread. The customer who ghosts after asking for a quote on a Sunday.
3. **Write like a tradie reads.** Short sentences. Plain English. Specific numbers. Australian spelling.
4. **Confident, not boastful.** We've seen the problem a hundred times. We know what works. No "industry-leading", no "world-class".
5. **Be definitive.** "Most tradies stop after one follow-up. That's why they lose jobs." Not "research suggests that..."

---

## Use these words

- Tradies, sparkies, plumbers, chippies, concreters, landscapers, fencers
- Quote, follow-up, chase, ghosted, cold lead, hot lead, win the job, lock it in
- The book, the calendar, the day, the run, on the tools
- Mate, your business, your customers
- ServiceM8, Tradify, simPRO, AroFlo, Jobber (real product names)

## Avoid these words

- Leverage, synergy, optimise (the noun), best-in-class, robust, scalable
- AI-powered, intelligent, smart (as a hype word), revolutionary, cutting-edge
- Solution provider, end-to-end, holistic, omnichannel
- Customer journey, funnel, touchpoint (technical)
- Disrupt, transform, empower, supercharge

> Exception: "touchpoint" is OK in technical/SEO contexts where it means a specific contact moment, e.g. "7+ touchpoints per quote".

## Founder positioning

The founder is positioned as a peer, not a guru. He:

- Has run trades businesses. (Or worked in/around them — be honest about which.)
- Knows what it's like to quote at the kitchen table at 9pm.
- Built this because he watched mates leave money on the table.
- Doesn't pretend to be a tech founder. The tech is just plumbing.

When the founder appears in copy:
- First person ("I built this because…") on the founder section.
- Third person elsewhere ("Built by [Name]…").
- Never "Mr." or full corporate titles.

## Headlines

The job of a headline is to make a tradie think *"that's me"* in under 2 seconds.

**Patterns that work:**
- "Stop losing jobs to slow follow-up." (problem-named, action verb)
- "It's not your prices. It's your follow-up." (reframe)
- "The leads aren't the problem. The follow-up is." (reframe)
- "Every quote chased. Every lead recovered." (outcome promise)

**Patterns to avoid:**
- "Empowering tradies through intelligent automation." (vague)
- "The future of trade business management." (corporate)
- "Revolutionise your customer experience." (jargon)

## CTAs

Two primary CTAs across the site:

- **Book a Strategy Call** — for ready-to-talk visitors
- **Get a Free Quote Follow-Up Audit** — softer entry, same booking flow

Secondary CTAs (use sparingly):
- "See how it works"
- "Read the blog"
- "Send a message"

Never:
- "Get started" / "Sign up" — we're not a SaaS
- "Learn more" — vague
- "Click here" — bad UX, bad SEO

## Numbers we lean on

These are repeatable across pages. If we use them, we use them consistently.

- **20–40%** lift in quote conversion within 90 days
- **<5 minutes** average follow-up time after quote sent
- **7+** touchpoints per quote (most tradies stop after 1)
- **6–10 hours/week** of admin saved per business
- **30–50%** of quotes go unfollowed-up by most tradies

If we ever change one, we update it everywhere (search the codebase for the figure).

## Industry-specific language

When writing for a vertical (fencing post, electrician post, etc.), use that trade's vocabulary:

- **Fencing:** runs, posts, panels, gates, Colorbond, palings
- **Electrical:** sparkies, switchboards, RCDs, compliance, callouts
- **Plumbing:** hot water, blocked drains, gas fitting, after-hours
- **Landscaping:** scopes, design, retaining, turf, irrigation
- **Building:** site, scope, variations, stages, settlement
- **Concreting:** pour, slab, footings, formwork, finish

This signals "we get your work" faster than any testimonial.

## Common mistakes to fix in review

- ❌ "Our innovative platform helps you…"
- ✅ "We chase your quotes for you so you can stay on the tools."

- ❌ "Drive efficiency in your customer engagement."
- ✅ "Every customer hears back from you the same day. Even if you're three deep in a slab."

- ❌ "Our AI handles your follow-up."
- ✅ "We set up automatic follow-up so quotes don't get forgotten."

- ❌ "Increase conversion by up to X%."
- ✅ "Most tradies we work with see 20–40% more quotes turn into jobs within 90 days."
